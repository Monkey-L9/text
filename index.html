The Development History Of Chinese Writing

Chinese characters came into being at the end of primitive society, with a history of 5000-6000 years. From the relatively mature oracle bone inscriptions, it has a history of more than 3000 years. Since the emergence of oracle bone inscriptions, the development of Chinese characters has gone through two stages: ancient characters and modern characters. The ancient characters stage can be divided into four stages: Oracle Bone Inscriptions, bronze inscriptions, big seal characters and small seal characters Regular script and other stages
















Oracle

Oracle bone inscriptions is an ancient Chinese writing, also known as "Qiwen", "oracle bone inscriptions", "Yin Ruins writing" or "tortoise shell and animal bone inscriptions". It is the earliest mature Chinese characters we can see, mainly refers to the characters carved on tortoise shells or animal bones by the royal family in the late Shang Dynasty. It is one of the earliest systematic Shang Dynasty characters known in China and East Asia. It was first found by the villagers of Xiaotun village in Anyang, Henan Province. At that time, they did not know that it was an ancient relic. It was only used as a medicine "keel" to cure all kinds of diseases. Many tortoise shells and animal bones engraved with oracle bone inscriptions were ground into powder, wasting a lot of valuable cultural relics In 1899, Wang Yirong discovered the location of the inscriptions on oracle bones from Anyang, Henan Province. Over the past hundred years, more than 154600 oracle bones have been unearthed through archaeological excavation and other means. In addition, oracle bone inscriptions also appeared in other areas of Henan and Shaanxi, which lasted from the late Shang Dynasty (about 1300 BC) to the spring and Autumn period. Oracle bone inscriptions have a symmetrical and stable pattern. It has three elements of calligraphy, namely, the use of pen, the formation of characters and the composition. In terms of the number and structure of fonts, oracle bone inscriptions have developed into a more rigorous system of characters. The "six characters" principle of Chinese characters is embodied in oracle bone inscriptions. However, the traces of the original pictures and characters are still more obvious, and the pictographic meaning is also more obvious.


Chinese Bronze Inscriptions

Jinwen is the name of a book style of ancient Chinese characters. Shang, Western Zhou, spring and autumn, Warring States period bronze inscriptions on the general term. The inscriptions can be divided into four types: inscriptions in Shang Dynasty (about 1300-1046 BC), inscriptions in Western Zhou Dynasty (about 1046-771 BC), inscriptions in Eastern Zhou Dynasty (770-222 BC) and inscriptions in Qin and Han Dynasties (221-219 BC). Bronze inscriptions of Shang Dynasty Even though there were bronzes before the Shang Dynasty, the beginning of inscriptions on bronze was really after pan Geng moved to Yin (now northwest Anyang, Henan). At the beginning, there were only a few figures. By the beginning of the Zhou Dynasty, it had reached more than 1200 words. At the end of Shang Dynasty, there were more and more bronzes with inscriptions on gold, but the description is still very simple, and most of them are taboos of the casters or their ancestors. Only when the Shang Dynasty died did articles appear. However, the longest article at that time was only 40 words. Bronze inscriptions of the Western Zhou Dynasty In the Zhou Dynasty, when the Shang Dynasty began, the inscriptions of Jin Dynasty gradually flourished. There are many records about the affairs of the emperor, such as the southern tour of King Zhao and the Western hunting of King Mu. Bronze inscriptions of the Eastern Zhou Dynasty Since King Ping moved to the East, more and more iron wares have been found, and more and more bronze music, such as bells, can be cast on the outside of bronze wares. Therefore, the inscriptions on the bronze wares are not just for princes and ministers, but also for military achievements and scales. At this time, the inscriptions were widely used, which can be called the heyday.


Lesser Seal Character

Xiaozhuan, after the first emperor of Qin unified the six states (222 BC), carried out the policy of "the same script, the same track" and unified weights and measures. The prime minister Lisi was responsible for the simplification and creation of a unified writing form of Chinese characters on the basis of the original use of Dazhuan in Qin. It was not until the end of the Western Han Dynasty (about 8 A.D.) that it was gradually replaced by Lishu. But because of its beautiful font and ancient style, it has always been favored by calligraphers. Because of its complex strokes, ancient forms, and can add twists and turns at will, seal engraving, especially the need for anti-counterfeiting official seal, has been using seal, until the collapse of the feudal dynasty, the emergence of modern new anti-counterfeiting technology. During the spring and Autumn period and Warring States period, the various Chinese characters appeared in various countries. After Qin Shihuang destroyed the six states, he ordered to unify the national characters according to the standard of "Xiaozhuan". Zhuan style, also known as "Zhuanshu", is a combination of Xiaozhuan and Dazhuan. Because it is customary to call "Zhenwen" Dazhuan, later generations often refer to "Zhuanwen" specifically for Xiaozhuan. The development of Chinese characters to Xiaozhuan stage, gradually began to shape (outline, stroke, structure), pictographic meaning weakened, making the text more symbolic, reducing the confusion and difficulties in writing and reading, this is also the first time in the history of our country to use administrative means to regulate the large-scale text. The Qin Dynasty used the sorted Xiaozhuan to unify the national characters, which not only basically eliminated the phenomenon of different characters in different places, but also greatly changed the situation of many different ancient characters, and played an important role in the development history of Chinese characters.

Regular Script

Regular script is also called regular script, real script and regular script. From the Lishu founded by Cheng Miao, it gradually evolved, more simplified, horizontal and vertical Cihai explains that it "has a square shape and straight strokes, which can be used as a model." So it's called regular script. It began in the late Han Dynasty and has been popular until modern times. At the end of Han Dynasty and the Three Kingdoms period, the writing of Chinese characters gradually changed from wave and line to Pai and Na, and there were strokes such as "side" (DOT), "Lue" (long PAI), "peck" (short PAI), "Ti" (straight hook), which made the structure more rigorous. For example, "Wuwei medical bamboo slips", "Juyan Han bamboo slips" and so on. The characteristic of regular script is that it is regular and neat. It is the model of font, so it is called regular script and has been used until modern times.








Cursive

Cursive script is a type of Chinese characters, with broad and narrow meanings. In a broad sense, regardless of the age, any scribbled words are regarded as cursive. In a narrow sense, as a specific font, it was formed in the Han Dynasty and evolved on the basis of Lishu for the convenience of writing. About from the Eastern Jin Dynasty, in order to be different from the new style cursive script at that time. The cursive script of Han Dynasty is called Zhangcao. The new style cursive script is relatively called "modern grass", which is also divided into big grass (also known as wild grass) and small grass. It feels beautiful in the madness. As Li Zhimin said: "in the pool, drink in reason, learn from things, get in the heart, understand in the image, and then enter the wonderful realm of cursive." Because the glyphs of cursive script are too simple and easy to be confused with each other, it can not replace the official script and become the main font as the official script replaces the seal script.










Running Script

Running script is a general term for calligraphy, which can be divided into running regular script and running cursive script. Based on regular script, it is a kind of font between regular script and cursive script, which is produced to make up for the slow writing speed of regular script and the illegibility of cursive script“ "Xing" means "walking", so it is not as scribbled as cursive, nor as upright as regular script. In essence, it is the cursive or regular script of regular script. Those with more regular script than cursive script are called "Xingkai", while those with more cursive script are called "xingcao". Running script has high practicability and artistry, while regular script is a kind of character symbol, which has high practicability and good skill; In contrast, cursive script is highly artistic, but its practicability is relatively insufficient.
